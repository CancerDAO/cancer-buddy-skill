# Access Pathways — China-Specific Expanded Access & Cross-Border Treatment

## 1. 临床急需药品临时进口

**Legal basis:** 《药品管理法》第65条
**Fast track:** 海南博鳌乐城先行区 (Boao Lecheng pilot zone) — pre-approved list of urgently needed drugs, bypasses standard NMPA registration.

**Application flow:**
1. Attending physician writes clinical necessity statement
2. Hospital submits application to 乐城管理局
3. Review (typically 5-15 working days for Boao; longer for non-Boao routes via provincial 药监局)
4. Import license issued → drug shipped → patient travels to Boao for dosing (or local hospital if non-Boao route)

**Required materials:**
- Diagnosis certificate + pathology/molecular evidence
- Prior treatment failure documentation
- Drug information (global approval status, prescribing info, clinical data)
- Informed consent (patient acknowledges unapproved status in China)
- Hospital qualification documents

**Key constraint:** Patient usually must receive treatment at the designated hospital (Boao). Some provinces (Beijing, Shanghai, Guangdong) have parallel pilot programs.

## 2. 拓展性临床试验 (Expanded Access / Compassionate Use)

**Legal basis:** GCP regulations (《药物临床试验质量管理规范》), NMPA guidance on expanded access.

**Flow:**
1. Identify drug + sponsor company
2. Contact sponsor's Medical Affairs / 医学部 (not sales)
3. Submit request with patient records demonstrating: (a) serious/life-threatening condition, (b) no alternatives, (c) patient meets basic safety criteria
4. Sponsor evaluates → issues approval letter
5. Site ethics committee (伦理委员会) reviews and approves
6. Drug shipped to site → dosing begins

**Communication letter template outline:**
- Patient demographics + diagnosis summary (1 paragraph)
- Treatment history + why standard options exhausted
- Molecular/biomarker rationale for requested drug
- Published evidence supporting use (citations)
- Specific request: drug name, proposed dose/schedule
- Physician contact info + institutional affiliation

**Tips:** Multi-national pharma (Roche, Pfizer, MSD, AZ) have established China Medical Affairs teams. Domestic biotechs (百济神州, 信达, 君实) are often more flexible. Response time: 2-8 weeks.

## 3. 研究者发起的试验 (IIT — Investigator-Initiated Trial)

**Approval:** Ethics committee only (no NMPA approval needed for off-label use of already-marketed drugs).

**Flow:**
1. Find willing PI (Principal Investigator) — must be 副主任医师 or above
2. PI writes protocol (can be N=1 or small cohort)
3. Hospital ethics committee review (2-4 weeks)
4. Drug procurement (hospital pharmacy or PI sources)
5. Treatment + data collection per protocol

**Advantages:**
- Most flexible pathway — protocol tailored to individual patient
- No sponsor dependency
- Can combine multiple agents or novel schedules
- Faster than formal expanded access

**Requirements:**
- Willing PI with sufficient rank and institutional backing
- Scientific rationale documented in protocol
- Ethics approval
- Drug supply chain (often the bottleneck)
- Patient bears cost unless PI has grant funding

## 4. 跨境医疗 (Cross-Border Treatment)

| Destination | Strength | Cost | Language Barrier | Visa Difficulty |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **US** | Cutting-edge trials, FDA expanded access, top oncology centers (MSKCC, MD Anderson) | $$$ | Medium (translators available) | High (B1/B2, may need invitation letter) |
| **Japan** | Proton/heavy ion therapy, quality care, cultural proximity | $$ | Medium (Chinese-speaking staff common) | Low |
| **Germany** | Lu-177 radiotherapy, precision oncology (Heidelberg), Sid's destination for Lu-177 FAP | $$$ | High | Medium (Schengen) |
| **South Korea** | Proton therapy, Samsung Medical, cost-effective | $$ | Low (large Chinese community) | Low |
| **Singapore** | English-speaking, bridge to global trials, Johns Hopkins/Duke partnerships | $$ | Low | Low |
| **Thailand** | Affordable, Bumrungrad International, medical tourism infrastructure | $ | Low | Low (visa-free) |
| **HK/Macau** | Access to drugs approved in HK but not mainland, no visa for 港澳通行证 holders | $$ | None | Very Low |

**Reference case:** Sid Sijbrandij flew to Germany for Lu-177 FAPI radiotherapy — demonstrates that some cutting-edge treatments are geographically locked.

**Practical steps:** Medical visa application → appointment booking (direct or via medical tourism agency) → medical records translation → treatment → follow-up plan for return to China.

## 5. 超说明书用药 (Off-Label Use)

**Flow:**
1. Attending physician proposes off-label use with evidence basis
2. Department/pharmacy committee review
3. Hospital ethics committee approval (部分医院简化流程)
4. Patient informed consent (must explicitly state off-label status)
5. Prescription + monitoring

**Key points:**
- Usually self-pay (医保 rarely covers off-label)
- Evidence hierarchy matters: NCCN guidelines mention > case series > mechanistic rationale
- Some hospitals have standing off-label committees; others require ad-hoc review
- Risk: physician liability if adverse outcome — informed consent is critical

## Cost Reduction Channels

| Channel | Details |
|---|---|
| **Patient Assistance Programs (PAP)** | Most major pharma offer: buy X cycles, get Y free. Apply via 中华慈善总会, 中国初级卫生保健基金会, etc. Eligibility: usually income-based. |
| **Charity funds** | 中国癌症基金会, 中国红十字基金会, disease-specific foundations. Application requires financial hardship proof. |
| **医保谈判药品** | Drugs on 国家医保目录 after negotiation — significant discount (often 50-80% off). Check latest 医保目录 version. |
| **惠民保 / 特药险** | City-level supplemental insurance (e.g., 沪惠保, 穗岁康). Covers some high-cost drugs not on 医保. Low premium (~100-200 RMB/year). Must purchase BEFORE diagnosis for most policies. |
| **CancerDAO community mutual aid** | Future feature: community-pooled resources, shared intelligence on cost optimization, group negotiation power. |

## Application Material Checklist

Prepare these for ANY expanded access / cross-border pathway:

- [ ] **Patient summary (bilingual CN/EN)**
  - Demographics, ECOG status, diagnosis with staging
  - Complete molecular profile (key mutations, biomarkers)
  - Treatment timeline with responses and progression dates
  
- [ ] **Medical necessity argument**
  - Why standard-of-care is exhausted or inappropriate
  - Scientific rationale for requested treatment (cite literature)
  - Expected benefit vs. risk assessment

- [ ] **Prior treatment failure evidence**
  - Imaging showing progression (RECIST criteria if available)
  - Lab trends demonstrating treatment failure
  - Physician statement on clinical deterioration

- [ ] **Informed consent points**
  - Unapproved/off-label status of treatment
  - Known and unknown risks
  - Alternative options (including no treatment)
  - Cost responsibility
  - Data collection/sharing terms (if part of IIT/trial)
  - Right to withdraw at any time
