# Sid Sijbrandij Case Analysis — Reference Benchmark

## Key Facts

- GitLab co-founder/CEO, net worth ~$400M at diagnosis
- Osteosarcoma, T5 vertebra (spine)
- Diagnosed November 2022
- Recurrence 2024 — standard care exhausted
- Achieved NED (No Evidence of Disease) via "founder mode" approach to cancer
- Site: osteosarc.com (25TB of medical data, publicly shared)

## What He Did

### Team
- Hired Jacob Stern as "Health CEO" — full-time chief of staff for his cancer
- Built advisory boards: computational biologists, oncologists, immunologists
- Treated cancer like a startup: hired talent, set OKRs, moved fast

### Diagnostics (Sid-level depth)
- Single-cell sequencing at 3 timepoints (pre-treatment, mid, post) — tracked tumor evolution in real-time
- WGS + WES + RNA-seq (3 different platforms for RNA to cross-validate)
- ctDNA/MRD serial monitoring
- Organoid drug sensitivity testing (grew his tumor in lab, tested drugs on it)
- TCR sequencing (tracked whether his immune system was learning to fight)

### Treatments
- **Lu-177 FAPI radiotherapy** — flew to Germany (not available in US/China), targeted FAP+ tumor cells
- Checkpoint inhibitors (anti-PD-1/PD-L1)
- Neoantigen peptide vaccine (custom-made from his tumor mutations)
- Oncolytic virus therapy
- mRNA vaccine (personalized)
- **5 FDA expanded access INDs filed in 48 hours** — unprecedented speed, required pharma connections + legal team
- CAR-T with logic gates as backup plan (multi-target to prevent escape)

### Result
- T-cell infiltration: 19% → 89% (massive immune activation)
- Achieved NED
- Documented everything publicly on osteosarc.com

## Cost Reality

- Total spend: millions of dollars (estimated $2-5M+)
- Full-time team: Jacob Stern salary + advisory board fees + research collaborations
- International travel: Germany for Lu-177, multiple US centers
- Organoid + single-cell + multi-omic testing: hundreds of thousands
- His quote: **"It costs $1B to get a drug approved. But it costs $1M to dose a single person."**

## Key Lesson

The barriers to Sid-level cancer care are **structural and organizational**, not technical:
- The knowledge exists (published literature, clinical data)
- The drugs exist (approved somewhere, or in trials)
- The diagnostics exist (commercially available)
- What's missing: someone to connect the dots, navigate the system, and move fast

**"The future is here, just not evenly distributed."** — William Gibson, but applies perfectly.

Beacon's thesis: an AI agent can perform 80-90% of the coordination work that Jacob Stern did, at <5% of the cost, making founder-mode cancer fighting accessible to non-billionaires.
